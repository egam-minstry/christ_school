About the School

Christ’s School, Ado Ekiti, is a prestigious government‑owned co‑educational secondary school in Ekiti State, Nigeria. Established in 1933 by Archdeacon Henry Dallimore of the Church Missionary Society (CMS), the school has produced outstanding leaders, neurosurgeons, governors, and academics. Our motto “Christus Victor” (Christ the Victor) drives excellence in academics, character, and service.

History of Christ’s School, Ado-Ekiti

Christ’s School was founded as a co‑educational institution by Archdeacon Henry Dallimore in 1933 on the site that now belongs to Ekiti Diocesan Anglican Headquarters. Originally conceived as model Ekiti CMS Central School, it was moved to its present site in 1936. During the commissioning of the School’s new premises in June 1936, the Governor Sir Bernard Bourdillon renamed the School as “CHRIST’S SCHOOL, ADO-EKITI”. Until 1941, the School only ran a two‑year Senior Elementary School course.

In 1942‑1943, the School was upgraded to Secondary Class status with Ven. Henry Dallimore as the High Master (Principal) and Mr. (later Bishop) J.A.I. Falope as First Master. In 1948, 7 ‘unofficial’ candidates were prepared and privately enrolled for the Senior Cambridge School Certificate Examinations – ALL PASSED. This feat enabled Form VI students to officially sit for Cambridge exams at Ilesha Grammar School. Rev. L.D. Mason arrived in 1948 and changed the title from High Master to Principal.

The former Ekiti Anglican Girl’s Secondary School (EAGSS), founded in 1955, was officially merged with Christ’s School in 1966. The union was dissolved in 1979, and the girls’ school became Christ’s Girls’ School. Over the decades, notable principals included Chief R.A. Ogunlade (first indigenous Principal), Mr. S.O. Agbebi, Chief Olusola Bayode, Mr. Kehinde Ojo (alumnus), and others. The school continues to receive tremendous support from the Alumni Association, Class Sets, and the State Government.
